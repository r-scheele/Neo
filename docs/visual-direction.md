# Visual Direction

## App Context

- App name: Neo
- Product category: AI-powered WhatsApp commerce operating system
- Target user: Nigerian WhatsApp-first SME owners and trusted staff
- Core outcome: Reply customers, capture orders, verify payments, review receipts safely, coordinate delivery, manage workflows, remember customers, recover sales, and stay in control of daily commerce operations.
- Emotional promise: Neo should feel like a calm, competent operations partner that helps the seller make good decisions under pressure.

## Mood

- Premium
- Calm
- Wise
- Trustworthy
- Warmly practical

Neo should feel human-first and business-serious. It should not feel like a playful chatbot, a crypto dashboard, a generic CRM, or a flashy AI product.

### Palette Rules

- Use warm ivory as the default app background, not pure white.
- Use deep forest green for primary actions and navigation, but avoid a green-only interface.
- Use muted gold sparingly for premium emphasis, never for body text.
- Status colors must include labels or icons. Do not rely on color alone.
- Error red should feel serious but not alarming.
- Avoid neon green, electric blue, purple AI gradients, and black-glass dashboard styling.

## Typography Feel

- Preferred feel: Humanist, editorial, readable, professional.
- Headings: Confident and restrained, with enough weight to anchor dense screens.
- Body: Clear, warm, and efficient. Avoid tiny gray text.
- Numbers and metrics: Tabular, compact, and highly legible.
- Tone: Businesslike but not cold. Use direct Nigerian commerce language where appropriate.

### Typography Rules

- Screen titles should be compact, not hero-sized.
- Dashboard numbers can be prominent, but should not dominate action queues.
- Use short labels for status and actions.
- Avoid decorative type, handwritten fonts, techy monospace branding, and overly rounded childish typography.

## Card Style

- Cards should be calm operational containers, not decorative tiles.
- Background: `#FFFDF8` or lightly tinted `#F1E7D6` for special callouts.
- Border: 1px sand border.
- Radius: 8px maximum for cards.
- Shadow: very subtle, soft, warm, and low elevation.
- Density: medium-high for lists and queues, medium for onboarding.

### Card Usage Rules

- Use cards for repeated queue items, approval items, receipts, orders, and customer summaries.
- Do not nest cards inside cards.
- Do not turn every section into a floating card.
- Do not use oversized marketing-style feature cards inside the app.
- Important approval cards should show the risk, recommended action, and decision buttons without visual clutter.

## Icon Style

- Style: Rounded outline icons.
- Stroke: Medium, visually around 1.75 to 2px.
- Corners: Rounded, friendly, professional.
- Fill: Avoid filled icons except for selected tab states or tiny status dots.
- Use: Navigation, actions, status, business types, approval categories.

### Icon Rules

- Standard UI actions should use familiar symbols: send, edit, check, x, search, filter, bell, receipt, message, package, user, settings.
- Business category icons can be custom but must stay simple at 24px.
- Avoid robot heads, sparkles-as-AI, lightning bolts, magic wands, and generic brain icons.

## Illustration Style

- Style: Premium editorial flat illustration with soft geometric shapes, subtle paper-like warmth, gentle shadows, and simple Nigerian commerce objects.
- Proportions: Slightly rounded and human, never childish.
- Lighting: Soft ambient lighting from upper left.
- Background: Transparent where useful, or warm ivory/simple branded scene.
- Visual motifs: chat bubbles, receipt slips, product cards, delivery pins, payment checkmarks, folded paper, woven texture hints, small leaf/commerce motif.

### Illustration Rules

- Use people only as simplified silhouettes or hands, not detailed faces unless intentionally approved.
- Use realistic commerce objects without fake readable text.
- Keep compositions uncluttered and readable at mobile size.
- Avoid robots, neon AI brains, holograms, generic dashboards, stereotypical Nigerian decoration, flags-as-decoration, currency flying everywhere, or busy marketplace scenes.

## Spacing Rules

- Base grid: 4px.
- Standard screen padding: 16px on small phones, 20px on larger phones.
- Tight inline gap: 4px.
- Small component gap: 8px.
- Row/content gap: 12px.
- Card internal padding: 14px to 16px.
- Section gap: 24px.
- Large onboarding gap: 32px.
- Minimum touch target: 44px.

### Layout Rules

- Queue screens should prioritize scan speed.
- Important action buttons should sit within thumb reach when possible.
- Long content should scroll naturally without hiding primary actions.
- Bottom navigation should stay simple and stable.
- Avoid desktop table layouts on mobile.

## Border Radius Rules

- Cards: 8px.
- Inputs: 8px.
- Buttons: 8px.
- Bottom sheets: 12px top radius.
- Pills, avatars, and tiny status chips: full radius.
- Receipt/image preview frames: 8px.

Do not use large bubbly radius for serious workflow components.

## Shadow Rules

- Default cards: border-first, shadow optional.
- Shadow color: warm charcoal with low opacity.
- Shadow size: small blur, low y-offset.
- Approval or receipt cards may use slightly stronger elevation, but still subtle.
- Avoid heavy floating shadows, glassmorphism, glow effects, and layered bokeh/orb backgrounds.

## Motion Style

- Motion personality: calm, fast, and purposeful.
- Transitions: 120ms to 220ms, ease-out.
- Use motion for: screen transitions, loading skeleton shimmer, approval confirmation, sent follow-up feedback, row expansion, pull-to-refresh.
- Success: small check animation or toast, not confetti.
- Error: gentle inline shake or highlight only when helpful.
- Avoid motion for: receipt review decisions, payment confirmations, and dense queue scanning.

## What To Avoid

- Neon AI visuals.
- Robot visuals.
- Generic dashboard styling.
- Purple/blue AI gradients.
- Crypto-finance dark dashboards.
- Loud stereotypical Nigerian decoration.
- Text inside generated images.
- Fake unreadable UI screenshots in illustrations.
- Overly playful mascots.
- Green-only palette.
- Cards nested inside cards.
- Desktop tables compressed into mobile screens.
- Auto-confirmation language around manual transfer receipts.
- Any visual implication that AI replaces human judgment.
